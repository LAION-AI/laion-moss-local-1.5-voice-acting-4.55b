Vanilla DramaBox TTS — Generate + Reward-Rank Benchmark

We took two-scene "CUT TO:" DramaBox stage-direction prompts (a voice/style description, a first scene of dialogue, a hard CUT TO:, then a second scene in a contrasting emotion) and ran them through the MOSS-TTS 8B voice-acting model with no reference audio. For every prompt we generate 4 different takes (4 random seeds), automatically score each take, and keep the best. This page reports how fast that is, how much quality you lose if you generate fewer than 4 takes, and what it would cost to scale to 10k / 1M prompts.

How a prompt is fed (the way the existing DramaBox pipeline does it): the spoken dialogue (the quoted lines) is given as the text to say; the surrounding two-scene stage direction (voice description, emotions, CUT TO:) is given as the style instruction with the literal quotes removed — leaving the quotes inside the instruction makes the 8B ramble and never stop (~0.02 clip/s, most takes dropped). Very long dialogues are capped to the first ~85 words so a take fits the model's ~57 s / 720-token window (median spoken length here ≈ 52s of audio).

The reward (how “best take” is decided)

For each group of 4 takes we compute three scores per take and min–max normalize each score WITHIN the group of 4 (so the worst take of the four = 0, the best = 1 on that axis), then add them:

reward = norm(invWER) + norm(genuineness) + norm(blend)

  • invWER = 1 − word-error-rate of an ASR transcript (NVIDIA Parakeet) vs the target text, after stripping parentheticals/vocal-bursts — higher = the words actually came out right.
  • genuineness (0–6) = a VoiceCLAP-commercial head rating how genuine/natural (not robotic) the delivery is.
  • blend (0–10) = a VoiceCLAP-commercial head rating overall commercial voice quality.
The 3 terms are added, not multiplied. Reward ranges 0–3. Ranking picks the highest-reward take of the 4.

5 · How to run it most efficiently

  • Measured throughput: 0.17 clips/s/A100 (≈ 149 groups/h) at bf16 + sdpa, group-of-4 batched.
  • Batch the seeds: the 4 seeds of a group are run as one generate() of batch-4 (repeat the input, sample per row). This is near-free vs batch-2 (section 1).
  • Length-bucket & cap: cap spoken text so a take fits the 720-token/~57s window; sizing max_new_tokens to the text avoids wasted decode steps.
  • sdpa attention (flash/mem-efficient kernels) + bf16; torch.inference_mode(); drop empty/runaway takes (rms & duration gate) so bad decodes don't reach scoring.
  • Shard across GPUs by group id — linear scaling, no cross-talk. Score on the same or separate GPUs (one VoiceCLAP encode feeds both genu & blend heads; Parakeet for invWER).
  • Prompt-gen on a separate GPU (gemma-3n-E4B bf16, vLLM, ~20k/h) fully overlaps TTS.

Directing character voices — Round 4: weighted dimensions & emotions, plus 3 new voices

Round 4 of the prompt-evolution study with MOSS-TTS-Local 4.55B, still zero-shot, no reference audio. This round introduces per-dimension and per-emotion reward weights so the search can chase the few signals that define a character, re-tunes four existing archetypes toward specific asks, and adds three brand-new voices.

What changed in round 4

The reward keeps the round-2/3 shape — 0.5·blend + 0.5·genu + w_vn·char_vn + w_emo·char_emo, min-max normalized per best-of-N call, times a WER factor — but char_vn and char_emo are now weighted means. Each archetype can assign a per-dimension weight (vn_w) and a per-emotion weight (emo_w), so the two or three signals that truly define a character count for more than the incidental ones.

The four re-tuned archetypes

• 👺 Evil Goblin — pushed from gleeful-cute toward sinister, cruel, sadistic menace; Malevolence/Malice ×2.5.
• 🐭 High-Pitch Mouse — pushed to an extreme high register; RANG ×2.5, REGS ×2.0, BRGT ×1.8.
• 😱 Pain Scream — real screams written into the text (‘Aaah! … Aaargh!’) and Arousal ×2.5.
• 🗯️ Ranting Fury — Arousal/intensity up-weighted further (AROU ×2.5, S_RANT ×1.8).

The three new archetypes

• 👻 Breathy Evil Ghost — airy hollow whispered menace, cold and sinister.
• 😢 Crying Woman — grief, sobbing, a breaking tearful voice.
• 😭 Crying Man — the same grief with a male voice cracking and choked sobs.

Each archetype was seeded (tuned ones from their round-3 champion, new ones authored fresh, ~10 candidates), evolved over several best-of-8 generations with programmatic mutation, picked in a same-call playoff, and finalized with best-of-64 — all on a single GPU. Every number below is re-loaded from the run summaries; the per-take weighted-dim values and the procedural caption are freshly re-predicted from each take's full 57-dim VoiceNet profile.

Round-4 lessons — weighted dims & emotions, and building new voices

  • Per-emotion weights are the sharpest new lever for ‘which feeling’. Up-weighting a single EmoNet head (Malevolence ×2.5 for the goblin, Sadness ×2.5 for the sad voices, Pain ×2.0 for the scream) reliably steered the search toward the intended affect without touching timbre. When one emotion defines the character, weight it heavily and let the others ride along at ×1.
  • Per-dimension weights concentrate char_vn where it matters. The mouse's RANG ×2.5 / REGS ×2.0 made the reward chase pitch height specifically, so the winner is measurably higher and squeakier; the scream's AROU ×2.5 pushed raw energy. Weighting 2-4 signature dims beats adding more unweighted dims — it stops a strong-but-off-target dim from dominating the mean.
  • Writing the affect into the TEXT does what the instruction alone cannot. Putting real screams (‘Aaah! ... Aaargh!’) in the spoken words made the pain-scream genuinely scream-like — vocalized interjections give the model something to actually scream, where a purely descriptive instruction over neutral words stays half-spoken. Keep a few real words so it is not pure noise (the ‘none’ WER gate allows it).
  • Malevolence is harder to raise than pitch or sadness. Even with Malevolence ×2.5, the goblin's absolute malice score stayed modest — the EmoNet malice head fires low on a nasal, giddy, cackling delivery; the up-weighting did lift it and push the prompt darker (sinister/sadistic/threatening), but a cute-shrill timbre and true menace pull against each other. Sadness and Fear were far easier to drive high.
  • The 3 new archetypes confirm the round-1→3 recipe transfers. Naming a concrete resonance/breath signature (breathy hollow whisper; audible sob/sniffle), a pitch/stance cue (low authority, trembling), and the target emotion in one prompt — then evolving with per-call normalization — produced clean ghost / crying-woman / crying-man voices from scratch, no reference audio.
  • Breath is a rewardable signature. The evil ghost leaned on S_WHIS + RESP (audible respiration): ‘all air and no body’, ‘breath rattling’ and (breathy hiss)/(hollow whisper) bursts raised the whisper/respiration dims and read as ethereal dread, while penalizing warmth/fullness/brightness kept it cold and hollow.
  • Same-call playoff still decides champions. As in every prior round, the highest single-generation candidate is not always the best under a fair head-to-head; re-scoring the strongest across generations under one normalization (best-of-8 playoff) then finalizing best-of-64 is what picks the published champion.
  • Gender is a cheap, reliable knob. Rewarding feminine gender (sad_woman) vs penalizing it (sad_man) split one grief prompt into two convincingly different voices — the GEND head responds strongly to a single ‘a man's voice’ / ‘softly feminine’ phrase.

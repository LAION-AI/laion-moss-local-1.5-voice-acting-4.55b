Directing character voices with emotion — Round 2

Round 2 of the prompt-evolution study over 10 character/style archetypes with MOSS-TTS-Local 4.55B, still zero-shot with no reference audio. This round adds an emotion-aware reward: alongside the VoiceNet character score, every take is now also scored by Empathic-Insight-Voice-Plus (EmoNet) for how strongly it conveys the archetype's target emotions.

What changed in round 2, for anyone

In round 1 we searched for the plain-English director instruction that best makes MOSS-TTS-Local become a character (a zombie, an ork, a fairy…), with no reference audio. The reward there scored only acoustic character — does it sound like the target — using 57 VoiceNet voice-property heads plus a VoiceCLAP blend score and a genuineness score.

Round 2 asks a harder question: does the voice also feel the right emotion? A perfect zombie rasp that sounds bored isn't a great zombie. So we added an emotion layer to the reward:

Empathic-Insight-Voice-Plus (EmoNet), in one line

EmoNet is LAION's speech-emotion model: a BUD-E-Whisper audio encoder feeds a bank of per-emotion regression heads that rate how strongly a clip expresses each of ~40 emotions (Anger, Helplessness, Amusement, Affection, Pain…). For each archetype we pick the handful of emotions that fit the character and average their scores into char_emo.

The round-2 reward, per best-of-N call:

reward = 0.5·norm(blend) + 0.5·norm(genu) + w_vn·char_vn + w_emo·char_emo (all min-max normalized within the call, then ×WER factor)

Compared with round 1's norm(blend) + norm(genu) + 1.5·char, round 2 makes three deliberate changes:

• Blend and genuineness are halved (1.0 → 0.5 each), so raw "sounds clean/real" pressure is reduced.
• Character is split and pushed harder: acoustic character char_vn is weighted 2.0 and the new emotion score char_emo is weighted 1.5 — together 3.5, dominating the reward.
• Prompts were pushed to be more extreme and emotional, seeded from the round-1 champions and re-optimized with the same evolutionary + best-of-N search.

Ranting was treated specially — weighted much higher (char_vn ×3.0, char_emo ×2.5) and prompted far more aggressively — because round 1 could not get furious enough without garbling the words.

Why champions are picked in same-call playoffs

The reward is min-max normalized within each best-of-N call, so a "2.4" in one generation is not comparable to a "2.4" in another. Champions were therefore chosen with shared-normalization playoffs — the finalists from different generations re-scored together in one call — not by comparing numbers across separate runs. Every metric below is a real re-loaded number; the procedural captions are freshly re-predicted from each take's 57-dim VoiceNet profile.

Consolidated round-2 lessons

  • Name the emotion, don't just describe the acoustics. Across all ten archetypes the single biggest round-2 lever was adding explicit feeling words (helpless / murderous glee / seething hatred / giddy delight / drowning in pain) on top of the round-1 timbre. char_emo jumped from ~0.3–0.4 (acoustics-only seeds) to 0.68–0.93, and those words map directly onto the EmoNet heads that the reward scores.
  • Emotion words must ride ON TOP of an intact acoustic core. Front-loading a heavy stack of affect adjectives (dragon g1c2, ork g1c10) craters VoiceCLAP blend and tanks reward. The winners keep the round-1 timbre description and append one emotion clause.
  • Character now dominates, so the optimum shifted toward emotional intensity. Halving blend+genu to 0.5 each while weighting char_vn 2.0 + char_emo 1.5 (=3.5) means a take can win with modest blend if both character scores are high. Best single-take rewards rose (dragon 2.46→3.10, pain_scream toward 3.65, ranting far higher).
  • Match the emotion words to the heads that actually fire. Leaning into the wrong emotion wastes the clause: Fatigue/Helplessness beat gore for zombie; Contempt beat raw Anger for dragon; Amusement (not malice) is goblin's strongest head; Interest was the decisive mouse lever.
  • One affect burst is still the default — except for high-char-weight archetypes. For zombie, dragon, pain_scream, fairy, mouse and both ASMR voices, a single emotion-matched burst won and a second burst hurt. But for ranting (char weight 3.0) a SECOND rage burst now pays off, reversing round 1 — the higher the character weight, the more stacked affect helps.
  • The ranting fix: shredding, not volume. "Maximum volume / ear-splitting" makes the model garble; reframing rage as "voice shredding and cracking at the breaking point" plus naming the contempt/hatred, plus a second burst, plus "the words still land," gave screaming fury (char 0.80, peaks 0.90) at invWER 1.0.
  • Alignment decides whether there's a tradeoff. Where the emotion aligns with the target timbre (ASMR softness+warmth+affection), pushing harder costs nothing — blend even rose. Where the archetype is inherently extreme (ork, goblin), higher character means sacrificing blend, which the halved weight now permits.
  • Per-call min-max normalization means champions must be picked in same-call playoffs. The reward is normalized within each best-of-N call, so raw rewards from different generations aren't comparable. Champions (goblin, dragon) were decided by shared-normalization playoffs, and several single-gen "winners" regressed to mid-pack when re-scored head-to-head.

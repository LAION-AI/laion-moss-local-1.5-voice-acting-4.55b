Directing character voices — Round 3: resonance, pitch & dominance

Round 3 of the prompt-evolution study over 10 character/style archetypes with MOSS-TTS-Local 4.55B, still zero-shot, no reference audio. Building on round 2's emotion-aware reward, this round explicitly exploits three more families of VoiceNet dimensions — resonance (chest / throat / nasal / head), pitch & register (how high/low, how wide the pitch swings) and dominance (commanding vs meek) — and teaches the prompts to name them in words.

What changed in round 3

The reward formula is unchanged from round 2 — 0.5·blend + 0.5·genu + w_vn·char_vn + w_emo·char_emo, min-max normalized per best-of-N call, times a WER factor. What changed is which VoiceNet dimensions feed char_vn: each archetype's positive/negative dim list was expanded to include the resonance, pitch/register and dominance heads that define the cliché.

The three new dimension families

• Resonance — where the voice vibrates: R_CHST (chest), R_THRT (throat), R_NASL (nose), R_HEAD (head). A zombie now wants a hollow chest groan plus throat rasp; a goblin wants a nasal buzz with no chest.
• Pitch & register — REGS (how high/low the voice sits) and RANG (how wide the pitch swings). Dragon = low register + narrow range; fairy/mouse = high register + wide range; the rant = violent swings.
• Dominance — S_AUTH (authoritative style) and STNC (dominant vs meek stance). Ork/dragon/rant want high; zombie/goblin/fairy/ASMR want low (meek).

Each archetype was re-seeded from its round-2 champion and mutated to name these properties explicitly ("a low hollow groan from a caved-in chest", "the pitch swinging violently high to low", "commanding and immovable" / "meek and powerless"), then re-optimized with the same evolutionary + best-of-N search on a single GPU. Champions were picked in same-call playoffs and finalized with best-of-64. Every metric below is a real re-loaded number; the per-take NEW dims row and the procedural caption are freshly re-predicted from each take's full 57-dim VoiceNet profile.

Round-3 lessons — resonance, pitch & dominance prompting

  • Resonance is a stackable knob, not a either/or. The zombie gained a low HOLLOW CHEST groan (R_CHST) on top of its wet throat rasp, and the ASMR man gained a soft warm chest UNDER the whisper — describing a second resonance cavity adds body without erasing the first. Say WHERE the sound resonates (chest / throat / nose / head), not just 'deep' or 'bright'.
  • Dominance words change perceived intensity, not just timbre. The single biggest round-3 win was reframing the RANT as domineering, browbeating, talking down with contempt (S_AUTH/STNC): char jumped ~0.50→0.88. 'Commanding / immovable / refusing to back down' also lifted ork and dragon. A furious voice that is also in charge scores as more furious.
  • But authority must ride ON TOP of the character core. Naming authority alone ('an orc warlord, commanding and dominant') collapsed the ork's character to ~0.30 and produced a clean commander, not a monster. Dominance words are an additive layer over the guttural/nasal/whisper timbre — never a replacement for it.
  • Meekness is a real, rewardable direction. The flip side — 'feeble, powerless, no authority' (low S_AUTH) — is what made the zombie read as a pitiful dying thing, the goblin as a sniveling underling, and both ASMR voices as gentle caregivers. Negating dominance is as useful as asserting it.
  • Pitch has two independent levers: register (how high/low) and range (how wide it swings). Dragon wanted LOW register + NARROW range ('subterranean register, flat and unwavering'); fairy/mouse wanted HIGH register + WIDE range ('top of the register, lilting/skittering'); the rant wanted a mid register with VIOLENT range. Name both — 'how high' and 'how much it moves' are different words that hit REGS vs RANG.
  • Wild pitch SWINGS encode mania and fury. 'The pitch swoops and cracks higher and shriller' (goblin), 'lurching from a low snarl up to a cracking shriek' (rant), 'skittering and leaping' (mouse) all raised RANG and the matching emotions. A flat delivery reads as calm no matter the words; movement reads as emotional.
  • Flat, narrow pitch encodes menace and authority. The inverse also holds: dragon and ork gained from 'low, flat, narrow, never leaping in pitch'. An unwavering low pitch reads as immovable and threatening — the opposite of the rant's chaos.
  • Some dims are already implied — check before piling on. Pain-scream's round-2 champion already had 'each cry cracking higher', so explicit octave-leap wording (RANG) added variance, not reward. Re-predict the champion's full profile first; if the target dim already fires, spend the words elsewhere.
  • Per-call normalization still rules — pick champions in same-call playoffs. As in round 2, the highest-emotion rant variant (r_domin, char_emo 0.95) lost the head-to-head playoff to the better-balanced r_stance. Always re-score finalists together under one normalization.

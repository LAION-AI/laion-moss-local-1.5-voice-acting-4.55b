Source taxonomies, made perceivable

Two LAION taxonomies define the space this cookbook maps. The Extension adds 11 situational axes (289 situations) that today's experts don't yet read; the Extreme-Physical set adds 60 high-intensity scenarios — violence, injury, accident, temperature, airway, startle. Every task below is annotated with the existing-expert superposition that approximates it and the new expert (usually one of these axes) that would read it cleanly.

The ranking idea

For any task, define its acoustic signature — the expected reading on each expert when the condition is genuinely present. Then score every candidate take by how closely it matches that signature, gated by the two experts that ask “how real does this sound?”: Genuineness and Vocal-Burst Blend.

score(take) = Σd wd·match(VoiceNet/EmoNet/burst[d], signature[d])  × Genuineness × Blend(if bursts) × invWER

Masking tasks flip it: the signal is the incongruence — a loud surface emotion with a calm body and low genuineness is exactly what “pretending to be angry” sounds like. Those we can already rank well.

★ The one that pays for itself: masking as incongruence

The hardest-seeming tasks — faking anger, hiding fear, fake-crying, forced emotion — are the ones our current stack ranks best, because “pretending” is not a feeling, it's a mismatch between layers. Surface emotion says one thing; the body and the Genuineness head say another. Measure the gap.

mask_score = EmoNet[surface emotion]  ×  (1 − Genuineness)  ×  |physiology − surface| e.g. fake anger = Anger↑ · low-genuineness · (arousal & tension stay LOW)

The same three-term structure detects suppressed laughter (amusement leak under control), nervous laughter (laugh-burst with low blend beside anxiety), and tears-of-joy (positive valence over a crying-breath). No new model — just the right product of the ones we have.
